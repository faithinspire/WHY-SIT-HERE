2873|||Credibility and Communication: Carrying the Message Well
2874|||Having a message is not enough. You must deliver it in a way that can be received.
2875|||The lepers had extraordinary news: the enemy has fled, the siege is over, there is food in abundance. But delivering that news required more than enthusiasm. It required credibility. It required communication. It required understanding how to speak truth in a way that would be believed.
2876|||"So they came and called unto the porter of the city: and they told them, saying, We came to the camp of the Syrians, and, behold, there was no man there, neither voice of man, but horses tied, and asses tied, and the tents as they were. 2877|||Notice the process:
2878|||They went to the gatekeepers (porters).
2879|||They reported what they had observed—facts, not embellishments.
2880|||The gatekeepers escalated the message to the king's house.
2881|||The message traveled through proper channels. It was delivered with specificity. It reached the decision-makers who could act on it.
2882|||This chapter is about how to carry the message God gives you in a way that actually lands. Because many well-meaning people sabotage their own message through poor delivery, damaged credibility, or communication that does not fit the audience.
2883|||The Message vs. The Messenger
2884|||Here is a hard truth: people often evaluate your message based on their perception of you.
2885|||This is not how it should be in a perfect world. Truth should be truth regardless of who speaks it. The 2+2=4 should be valid whether a professor says it or a child.
2886|||But we do not live in a perfect world. In this world, credibility matters. Character matters. Reputation matters. How you have lived affects how your words are received.
2887|||The lepers had a credibility problem. They were outcasts. They were unclean. They had no social standing. If anyone in Samaria should have been dismissed, it was them.
2888|||Yet their message got through. How?
2889|||1. They did not exaggerate.They did not say, "The camp is full of gold and treasure beyond imagination!" They said, "No man there… horses tied, asses tied, tents as they were." Observation. Fact. Description.
2890|||When you deliver good news, do not oversell it. Let the truth speak for itself. Exaggeration creates suspicion.
2891|||2. They went through proper channels.They did not sneak into the city and start shouting. They went to the gatekeepers—the people whose job it was to receive and escalate information. They worked within the system.
2892|||When you carry a message, consider who needs to hear it and through what channel. Going around proper structures can undermine even a true message.
2893|||3. They spoke humbly, not arrogantly.They did not say, "We are the saviors of this city!" They simply reported what they had found. The focus was on the news, not on themselves.
2894|||When you share what God has done, keep yourself small and the message big. Self-promotion distorts testimony.

3011|||Four years of applications. Four years of interviews that led nowhere. Four years of watching her savings deplete, her confidence erode, her hope flicker.
3012|||She had two degrees. She had experience. She had references. But the economy was harsh, her industry was contracting, and doors kept closing.
3013|||By year three, she had stopped believing. She went through the motions—applying, interviewing—but inside, she expected nothing. She had accepted that this was her life now. She would survive, but she would not thrive.
3014|||Then, in a single week, everything changed.
3015|||An old contact reached out about a role in a different industry—one Amara had never considered. She interviewed out of desperation rather than expectation. To her shock, they offered her the job. The salary was higher than any she had ever earned. The role was a perfect fit for skills she did not know she had.
3016|||Within 30 days, she went from four years of unemployment to a position that exceeded her wildest prayers.
3017|||"I still do not fully understand it," Amara says now. "I had given up. I had accepted the famine as permanent. Then, overnight, everything turned. I did not deserve it. I had not done anything extraordinary. It just... turned. Like God decided it was time, and nothing could stop it."
3018|||Joseph: Thirteen Years to One Morning
3019|||Joseph's story is one of the most dramatic examples of sudden turnaround in Scripture.
3020|||At seventeen, he was sold into slavery by his own brothers. He spent years in Potiphar's house, then years in prison on false charges. For over a decade, his life seemed to be moving in one direction: downward.
3021|||Then, in a single day, everything turned.
3022|||Pharaoh had a dream. None of his magicians could interpret it. Someone remembered Joseph—the Hebrew prisoner who could interpret dreams. Joseph was brought from the dungeon. He interpreted the dream. Pharaoh was so impressed that he made Joseph second-in-command over all Egypt.
3023|||"And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, See, I have set thee over all the land of Egypt. And Pharaoh took off his ring from his hand, and put it upon Joseph's hand, and arrayed him in vestures of fine linen, and put a gold chain about his neck." (Genesis 41:41-42, KJV)
3024|||From prison to palace. From forgotten prisoner to prime minister. In one day.
3025|||Thirteen years of waiting. One morning of turning.
3026|||The wait was long. The turn was sudden.
3027|||The Officer Who Missed the Turn
3028|||There is a warning embedded in the 2 Kings 7 story.
3029|||When Elisha prophesied the turnaround, a royal officer scoffed: "Behold, if the LORD would make windows in heaven, might this thing be?" (2 Kings 7:2).
3030|||He could not believe it. The situation was too far gone. The math did not work. Even God could not fix this—or so he thought.
3031|||Elisha's response was chilling: "Behold, thou shalt see it with thine eyes, but shalt not eat thereof" (2 Kings 7:2).
3032|||And so it happened. When the city rushed to plunder the Syrian camp, the officer was assigned to manage the gate. The crowd, desperate for food, trampled him to death.
3033|||He saw the turnaround. He did not taste it.
3034|||Cynicism can disqualify you from the very miracle you witness.
3035|||This officer was not punished for asking a question. He was punished for mocking the word of God. His skepticism was not humble curiosity—it was arrogant dismissal. And his arrogance cost him his life.
3036|||When God speaks a word of turnaround over your life, do not mock it. Your natural mind may not see how it could happen. Your circumstances may seem too far gone. The math may not work. But God is not limited by what you can calculate.
3037|||Believe the word. Position yourself for the turn. Do not be the officer who sees but does not taste.
3038|||Another Story: The Business That Turned on a Phone Call
3039|||Chukwuemeka had started a small IT services company seven years before. For most of those years, the company had struggled. Clients were few. Cash flow was tight. He had considered closing multiple times.
3040|||By year six, he was exhausted. He had poured everything into this business—his savings, his time, his reputation. And it was not working. He began planning his exit. He would close the company, get a regular job, and chalk this up to a failed dream.
3041|||Then his phone rang.
3042|||A friend of a friend had recommended his company to a large corporation that was looking for a local IT partner. They had seen his profile online, liked his background, and wanted to meet.
3043|||Within two months, Chukwuemeka had signed a contract worth more than everything his company had earned in the previous six years combined. That one contract stabilized the business. It led to referrals. It built credibility. It opened doors that had been locked for years.
3044|||"I was one month from closing," Chukwuemeka says. "One month. If that call had come 60 days later, I would have been done. I would have missed my own harvest because I gave up too soon."
3045|||The Temptation to Give Up Too Soon
3046|||Here is a danger that kills many destinies: giving up just before the turn.
3047|||Galatians 6:9 says, "And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."
3048|||Notice the conditional: "if we faint not." The reaping is promised—but only to those who do not faint. Those who give up just before harvest will never taste the fruit.
3049|||How many businesses have closed months before their breakthrough? How many students have dropped out semesters before their degree? How many couples have divorced weeks before their reconciliation? How many believers have lost hope days before their deliverance?
3050|||You do not know how close your turn is. The lepers could not see that the Syrian camp was already empty. They did not know that their breakthrough was waiting just a short walk away. All they knew was that staying at the gate meant certain death. So they moved.
3051|||If you are exhausted, I understand. If you are discouraged, I understand. If you have been waiting for years and nothing has changed, I understand.
3052|||But do not give up yet. The economy can turn overnight. Your phone could ring tomorrow. An email could arrive next week. A connection could be made next month. A door could open next year.
3053|||Do not let weariness disqualify you from the harvest that is coming.
3054|||Positioning for the Turn
3055|||While you cannot control when the turn comes, you can control whether you are positioned to receive it.
3056|||1. Stay faithful in the small things.Joseph was faithful in Potiphar's house and in prison. He did not slack because his circumstances were unjust. His faithfulness positioned him to be remembered when Pharaoh needed an interpreter.
3057|||2. Keep developing.While waiting for the turn, do not stop growing. Build skills. Deepen relationships. Increase knowledge. When the door opens, be ready to walk through it.
3058|||3. Stay connected.Breakthrough often comes through relationships. The phone call that changed Chukwuemeka's business came through a friend of a friend. Stay connected to people. Do not isolate in your struggle.
3059|||4. Maintain hope.Cynicism and despair close doors that hope keeps open. The officer who mocked Elisha disqualified himself through his attitude. Guard your heart against bitterness.
3060|||5. Keep moving.The lepers did not sit at the gate hoping the camp would come to them. They rose and walked. Movement positions you for discovery.
3061|||The Suddenness of God Throughout Scripture
3062|||The economy turning overnight is not an isolated event. It is a pattern in Scripture:
3063|||The Red Sea parted suddenly — Israel was trapped. Pharaoh's army was approaching. There was no way out. Then Moses stretched his rod, and in one night, the sea parted, Israel crossed, and Egypt drowned.
3064|||Jericho fell suddenly — Israel marched for seven days. Nothing visible happened for six days. Then, on the seventh day, after the seventh circuit, at the sound of the shout, the walls collapsed instantly.
3065|||Peter was released from prison suddenly — He was bound in chains, sleeping between two soldiers, behind multiple locked gates. An angel appeared, chains fell off, doors opened by themselves, and Peter walked out free.
3066|||The Holy Spirit came suddenly — The disciples had waited for days. Then, "suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind" (Acts 2:2). Tongues of fire. Languages of the world. Three thousand saved. The church was born.
3067|||God specializes in sudden.
