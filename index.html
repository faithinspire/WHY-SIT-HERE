About the Book

Breaking the Trap of Stagnation — Spiritually, Financially, and Personally

Four lepers sat at the gate of Samaria, surrounded by famine, siege, and certain death. They had every reason to stay. They had every excuse to remain. But one of them asked a question that changed everything: "Why sit we here until we die?"

In this prophetic and practical book, Pastor Olushola Odunuga Paul unpacks the ancient story of 2 Kings 7 to reveal the timeless traps that keep people — believers, leaders, and dreamers — locked in seasons of stagnation, fear, and spiritual paralysis.

This is not a motivational book. This is a confrontation. A divine summons to rise from the gate of your own limitations and walk toward the abundance that God has already prepared in the camp of your enemy.

Whether you are stuck in a career, a ministry, a relationship, a financial rut, or a spiritual wilderness — this book will not let you sit still.

The Author

Olushola Odunuga Paul

Lead Pastor, Ruach Apostolic Center · Lagos, Nigeria

Olushola Odunuga Paul is a pastor, teacher, and prophetic voice whose ministry has touched thousands across Nigeria and beyond. As the Lead Pastor of Ruach Apostolic Center in Lagos, he has built a community marked by the Word, the Spirit, and a relentless pursuit of purpose.

Known for his ability to bridge the ancient text with the modern struggle, Pastor Olushola brings the same fire to the pulpit that he brings to the page — confronting comfort, dismantling excuses, and calling people into the fullness of what God has prepared for them.

Why Sit Here and Die? is his most urgent message yet — a book born from years of watching gifted people remain at the gate of their own potential, waiting for a permission that God already gave them.

"God is not waiting for you to be ready. He is waiting for you to move. The lepers didn't have a plan — they had a decision."
